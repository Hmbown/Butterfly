# Visual Storyboard

Use this sequence for public-facing explanations. Each visual has one purpose
and one evidence anchor. All four assets are regenerated by
`scripts/render_butterfly_assets.py` from the tracked benchmark/quality JSON.

## Visual 1 — Headline ladder (hero)

- File: `docs/assets/butterfly_ladder.png`
- Command:
  ```bash
  /Volumes/VIXinSSD/butterfly/.venv-macos-metal/bin/python scripts/render_butterfly_assets.py
  ```
- Purpose: show the win as a function of context, on both 0.8B and 4B
  4-bit MLX. Three panels per row — e2e ratio, peak ratio, retained-KV
  ratio — make it obvious which dimensions improve and where the
  crossover sits.
- Claim supported: the win grows monotonically with context; compressed
  Butterfly beats stock on **every** metric by 128 k.
- Evidence source: `results/benchmarks/qwen35_0p8b_mlx/pA3_*` and
  `results/benchmarks/qwen35_4b_mlx/`.
- Use as: README hero, top of `BUTTERFLY_POSITIONING.md`.

![Headline ladder](assets/butterfly_ladder.png)

## Visual 2 — Selector substitution (V4 vs Butterfly)

- File: `docs/assets/butterfly_vs_v4.png`
- Purpose: make the unique-vs-derivative argument legible at a glance.
  Left column: V4's learned Lightning Indexer (3 trained projections +
  ReLU top-k). Right column: Butterfly's deterministic `causal_shift`
  adjacency (zero learned parameters). The substitution is the entire
  retrofit story.
- Claim supported: Butterfly is V4's systems shape with the load-bearing
  selector swapped for a fixed graph; that's why it drops in on a frozen
  pretrained checkpoint and V4 cannot.
- Evidence source: DeepSeek V4 paper §2.3.1 (eqs. 13–17),
  `bna/topology/butterfly.py`.
- Use as: §C "What makes it Butterfly, not V4" anchor.

![Selector substitution](assets/butterfly_vs_v4.png)

## Visual 3 — causal_shift topology

- File: `docs/assets/butterfly_topology.png`
- Purpose: show how a single layer's adjacency (one partner per query
  block) composes across stages into full causal-prefix coverage at
  depth ⌈log₂ N⌉.
- Claim supported: structural mixing — log-depth between any two blocks
  through the union of per-stage adjacencies.
- Evidence source: `bna/topology/butterfly.py:butterfly_partner_block`,
  `bna/mlx/attention.py:build_block_butterfly_layout`.
- Use as: anchor for the topology section in the README and in any
  paper figure for the proof story.

![causal_shift adjacency](assets/butterfly_topology.png)

## Visual 4 — Quality smoke card

- File: `docs/assets/butterfly_quality_card.png`
- Purpose: highlight the single strongest token-level parity result —
  the 4B 1 k row, where compressed greedy decode is 32/32 identical to
  stock and top-1 agrees — and put it next to the (more pessimistic)
  longer-context behavior so the claim boundary is visible.
- Claim supported: compressed Butterfly does not silently break the
  model at short context on a real-size checkpoint; quality drift at
  longer context is fractional and bounded.
- Evidence source: `results/benchmarks/qwen35_0p8b_mlx/quality_smoke_*/compare.json`,
  `results/quality/qwen35_4b/compare_*.json`.
- Use as: directly under the ladder in any external write-up.

![Quality smoke](assets/butterfly_quality_card.png)
